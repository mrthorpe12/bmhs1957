Madrid

From Barcelona, I traveled via train to Madrid, the capital of Spain. Home to over 3 million residents, Madrid's stately avenues, grand parks, and world-renowned museums give it a cosmopolitan feel.

While in Madrid, one must-see destination is the Plaza Mayor. Built in the late 16th century during the reign of Philip III, the Plaza Mayor is a vibrant hub within the old city center, home to upscale restaurants and shops.[1]

Another point of interest is the Palacio Real, or Royal Palace. Home to the Spanish royal family, the palace is open for guided tours and is a major attraction.

Leisure opportunities are never far. The city's streets, parks, and gardens attracts visitors and locals alike.
